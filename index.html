01 About

Why finance, and
why this way.

I read Economics, Finance & International Business at Oxford Brookes — currently a 3.6/4.0 GPA, on track for First-Class Honours — and have just completed an exchange year at Université Dauphine in Paris, one of the top economics faculties in continental Europe. I came up through Charterhouse with A-levels in Mathematics, Economics and French, and the DELF B2 in French.

Finance is where the two things I care about most collide: understanding markets and building the analytical infrastructure to read them faster than anyone else. In Q4 2024 I led the Oxford Brookes team in the Bloomberg Global Trading Challenge — a global competition of 10,240 students across 396 universities — closing the window +16.4% across 24 trades on a $1M Bloomberg Terminal book, outperforming the WLS Index benchmark. That experience crystallised the thesis behind everything I’ve built since: directional conviction is multiplied by tooling.

I want to operate at the intersection of equity research, quantitative methods, and financial technology — whether that’s a buy-side desk, a sell-side research team, or a quantitatively-driven asset manager. The two projects on this page exist because I needed them: a multi-regime futures-trading research framework powered by Claude and Alpaca’s paper API, and a natural-language SQL finance analyser I use to interrogate my own data. I built them, document them, and stand behind every line of code.
